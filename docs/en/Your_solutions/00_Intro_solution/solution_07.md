# Section 0 - Mathematical Foundations
## 7. Logic & Series

A bicycle is 10 meters from a wall and moves toward it at a constant speed of 1 m/s.  
A fly starts from the bicycle's front wheel and flies toward the wall at 2 m/s.  
When it hits the wall, it instantly turns back and flies toward the bicycle, and so on.

We need to find the total distance the fly travels before the bicycle reaches the wall.

---

## Step 1: Time until the bicycle reaches the wall

Distance to the wall:

d = 10 meters

Speed of the bicycle:

v = 1 m/s

Time needed:

t = distance / speed

t = 10 / 1

t = 10 seconds

---

## Step 2: Distance traveled by the fly

The fly flies continuously during this entire time.

Speed of the fly:

v_fly = 2 m/s

Time:

t = 10 s

Distance traveled:

distance = speed × time

distance = 2 × 10

distance = 20 meters

---

## Final Result

The total distance traveled by the fly is:

20 meters
