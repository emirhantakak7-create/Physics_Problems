## 8. Definite Integrals

We need to calculate the area under the curve of the function:

f(x) = sin(x)

from x = 0 to x = π.

This area is given by the definite integral:

∫₀^π sin(x) dx

---

## Step 1: Find the antiderivative

The antiderivative of sin(x) is:

∫ sin(x) dx = -cos(x)

So:

∫₀^π sin(x) dx = [-cos(x)]₀^π

---

## Step 2: Evaluate at the limits

Substitute x = π and x = 0:

[-cos(x)]₀^π = -cos(π) - (-cos(0))

Now use the known values:

cos(π) = -1  
cos(0) = 1

So:

-cos(π) - (-cos(0)) = -(-1) - (-1)

= 1 + 1

= 2

---

## Final Result

The area under the curve is:

2
