# Section 0 - Mathematical Foundations
## 5. Trigonometry

A vector has a magnitude of 15 and makes an angle of 60° with the horizontal axis.

We need to calculate the horizontal and vertical components of the vector.

---

## Step 1: Component formulas

For a vector with magnitude V and angle θ with the horizontal axis:

Horizontal component:

Vx = V cos(θ)

Vertical component:

Vy = V sin(θ)

---

## Step 2: Substitute the values

Magnitude:

V = 15

Angle:

θ = 60°

So:

Vx = 15 cos(60°)

Vy = 15 sin(60°)

---

## Step 3: Use trigonometric values

cos(60°) = 1/2

sin(60°) = √3 / 2

---

## Step 4: Calculate the components

Horizontal component:

Vx = 15 × (1/2)

Vx = 7.5

Vertical component:

Vy = 15 × (√3 / 2)

Vy = (15√3) / 2

---

## Final Components

Horizontal component:

Vx = 7.5

Vertical component:

Vy = (15√3) / 2
