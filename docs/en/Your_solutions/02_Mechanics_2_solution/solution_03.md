# 3. Conservation of Energy

A pendulum of length L = 1.0 m is released from rest at an initial angle of theta = 15°.
We need to find the speed of the bob at the lowest point of its swing.

Given:
Length, L = 1.0 m
Initial angle, theta = 15°
Acceleration due to gravity, g = 9.81 m/s^2
Initial velocity, v_i = 0 m/s

---

## Step 1: Calculate the change in height (h)

When the pendulum is pulled back by an angle theta, it rises by a vertical height `h` relative to its lowest point.
Using basic trigonometry, the formula for this vertical height is:
h = L - L * cos(theta)
h = L * (1 - cos(theta))

Substitute the given values:
h = 1.0 * (1 - cos(15°))
Using cos(15°) ≈ 0.9659:
h = 1.0 * (1 - 0.9659)
h = 0.0341 m

---

## Step 2: Apply Conservation of Energy

At the release point, the pendulum has maximum Potential Energy (PE) and zero Kinetic Energy (KE).
At the bottom of the swing, all PE has been converted entirely into KE.
PE_initial = KE_final
m * g * h = (1/2) * m * v^2

Notice that the mass `m` cancels out from both sides of the equation:
g * h = (1/2) * v^2

---

## Step 3: Solve for the final velocity (v)

Rearrange the equation to solve for the maximum speed `v`:
v^2 = 2 * g * h
v = sqrt(2 * g * h)

Substitute the known values:
v = sqrt(2 * 9.81 * 0.0341)
v = sqrt(0.669)
v ≈ 0.818 m/s

---

## Final Result

The speed of the pendulum bob at the bottom of its swing is approximately **0.818 m/s**.
