# 2. Harmonic Motion

A mass-spring system oscillates according to the given position function:
x(t) = 0.2 * cos(10 * pi * t)

Given parameters:
Mass, m = 10 kg
From the standard harmonic motion equation x(t) = A * cos(w * t), we can identify:
Amplitude, A = 0.2 m
Angular frequency, w = 10 * pi rad/s

---

## Step 1: Calculate the spring constant (k)

The relationship between angular frequency, mass, and the spring constant is:
w = sqrt(k / m)

Squaring both sides and solving for k:
k = m * w^2
k = 10 * (10 * pi)^2
k = 10 * 100 * pi^2
k = 1000 * pi^2

Using pi ≈ 3.14159:
k ≈ 1000 * 9.8696
k ≈ 9869.6 N/m

---

## Step 2: Calculate the total mechanical energy (E)

The total mechanical energy in a simple harmonic oscillator is constant and is equal to the maximum potential energy (when the spring is fully stretched to its amplitude A):
E = (1/2) * k * A^2

Substitute the known values:
E = 0.5 * (1000 * pi^2) * (0.2)^2
E = 0.5 * 1000 * pi^2 * 0.04
E = 500 * pi^2 * 0.04
E = 20 * pi^2

Using pi ≈ 3.14159:
E ≈ 20 * 9.8696
E ≈ 197.39 Joules

---

## Final Result

- The spring constant `k` is exactly **1000π^2 N/m** (approx. 9869.6 N/m).
- The total mechanical energy `E` is exactly **20π^2 Joules** (approx. 197.39 J).
