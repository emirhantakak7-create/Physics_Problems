# 8. Circular Motion

We need to calculate the centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator.
Given: Radius of the Earth, R = 6378 km

---

## Step 1: Convert to standard SI units

First, we convert the radius into meters:
R = 6378 km = 6,378,000 m

Next, we need the period of the Earth's rotation (T) in seconds. The Earth completes one full rotation in 24 hours:
T = 24 hours * 60 minutes/hour * 60 seconds/minute
T = 86,400 s

---

## Step 2: Calculate the angular velocity

Angular velocity (`omega`) is the angle covered per unit time:
omega = 2 * pi / T
omega = 2 * 3.14159 / 86400
omega ≈ 7.272 * 10^-5 rad/s

---

## Step 3: Calculate the centripetal acceleration

The formula for centripetal acceleration is:
a_c = omega^2 * R
a_c = (7.272 * 10^-5)^2 * 6,378,000
a_c = (5.288 * 10^-9) * 6,378,000
a_c ≈ 0.0337 m/s^2

*(Note: Using the tangential velocity method, v = 2*pi*R/T ≈ 463.8 m/s, and a_c = v^2/R gives the exact same result).*

---

## Final Result

The centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator is approximately **0.0337 m/s^2**.
