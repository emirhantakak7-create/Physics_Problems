# 9. Momentum Comparison

We need to compare the momentum of a fly and a tennis ball.
The formula for momentum is `p = m * v`.

Given for the fly:
Mass, m_fly = 2 g
Velocity, v_fly = 10 m/s

Given for the tennis ball:
Mass, m_ball = 60 g
Velocity, v_ball = 1 m/s

---

## Step 1: Convert mass to standard SI units (kg)

For proper physics calculations, we must convert grams to kilograms (divide by 1000):
m_fly = 2 / 1000 = 0.002 kg
m_ball = 60 / 1000 = 0.060 kg

---

## Step 2: Calculate the momentum for both objects

**For the fly:**
p_fly = m_fly * v_fly
p_fly = 0.002 kg * 10 m/s
p_fly = 0.020 kg*m/s

**For the tennis ball:**
p_ball = m_ball * v_ball
p_ball = 0.060 kg * 1 m/s
p_ball = 0.060 kg*m/s

---

## Step 3: Compare the results

Now we compare the two values:
0.060 kg*m/s > 0.020 kg*m/s
p_ball > p_fly

The tennis ball's momentum is three times greater than the fly's momentum.

---

## Final Result

The **60-gram tennis ball** moving at 1 m/s has greater momentum (0.06 kg*m/s) than the 2-gram fly moving at 10 m/s (0.02 kg*m/s).
